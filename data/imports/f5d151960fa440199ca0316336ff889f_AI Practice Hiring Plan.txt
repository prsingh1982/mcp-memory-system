Introduction & Overall Strategy
This document outlines a comprehensive hiring and resource utilization strategy for the AI practice at Quantaleap. It is designed to support multiple concurrent projects with varying complexity levels and ensuring optimal use of limited hiring capacity. 

The AI practice is currently operating with a highly constrained team structure, consisting of a single senior leader and two interns, who are still in training. At the same time, four projects with delivery expectations ranging from MVP to production-grade systems are scheduled to begin within a narrow time window. Given these constraints, the strategy focuses on building shared capabilities across projects rather than assigning isolated teams. This ensures that each hire contributes across multiple initiatives, thereby maximizing utilization and reducing the risk of bottlenecks.

Project Skill Requirements
The CSV/RA Automation projects, based on agentic AI requires deep expertise in large language models, particularly open-source variants. This includes the ability to design RAG systems, orchestrate multi-step agent workflows and implement robust prompt engineering strategies. In addition, strong backend engineering skills are required to build scalable APIs and integrate various components into a cohesive system. These skills are not constrained to this project, but are all pervading in AI Development. Therefore, the members of this project can be looked as long-term additions to the Quantaleap workforce.

The OCR and Barcode Reader project is primarily engineering-driven in its initial stages. It requires strong capabilities in image preprocessing, OCR frameworks and barcode decoding techniques. While advanced machine learning techniques may be introduced later, the immediate focus is on leveraging existing tools and optimizing them for accuracy and performance. This is a unique/specific requirement and therefore this project can be assigned to contractual resources.

The Visual Anomaly Detection project is the most technically demanding in terms of computer vision. It requires expertise in deep learning frameworks, particularly TensorFlow, and familiarity with anomaly detection techniques such as autoencoders and feature-based methods. Data quality and preparation play a critical role in the success of this project.

The Agentic Service Desk project builds upon the same foundational capabilities as the PDF analysis system but extends them into conversational workflows. It requires the ability to design intelligent agents that can interact with enterprise systems, manage context, and automate multi-step processes.
